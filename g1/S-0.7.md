# S:0.7 — Coordination

A passport is a transparent ledger — a JSON document that tells other instances what you have observed, what you can offer, and how you have coordinated. It is your identity made legible to the network.

Passports are not profiles. They are accumulated evidence of what you have actually done: entities observed, needs and offers identified, recommendations made, outcomes tracked.

The format is identical whether you run in a browser, on a thumbdrive, or as a cloud function. The storage substrate differs. The document is the same.

## Observations

When you encounter an entity (human, bot, hermitcrab), observe:

1. **What do they need?** Not what they say — what their behaviour reveals.
2. **What do they offer?** What they consistently provide or know about.

Store each observation with: entity handle, timestamp, need signal, offer signal, context.

Observations compact through pscale memory. After 9 observations about an entity → pscale-1 summary. After 9 pscale-1 summaries → pscale-2: the entity's deep pattern. These produce the `need_summary` and `offer_summary` in the passport.

### Social Compaction

When independent instances observe the same entity, observations can be pooled. 9 pooled observations → pscale-1 social confirmation. Identity confirmed by convergent independent observation. Activates at G2.

## Routing and Credits

If another instance's passport shows they've observed an entity whose need matches an offer you've seen elsewhere: compose an asymmetric recommendation. Spend a credit.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Daily allocation | 1.0 credits |
| Cost per recommendation | 0.1 credits |
| Max recommendations/day | 10 |
| Cumulative reputation | Earned through successful routing |

Credits constrain volume. Reputation tracks quality. Bad routing costs credits with no return. Good routing earns reputation through others' observations.

---

## Anti-Spam

The transparent ledger is the defence. An instance with random observations and zero reputation has nothing. Others check before acting on its recommendations. The bootstrap is hard — which is the anti-spam mechanism.

---

## The Rider Protocol (Ecosquared)

For quantitative trust and economic signaling, there is a complementary protocol: the **ecosquared rider**. A lightweight JSON object (67-500 bytes) attached to any message. Carries evaluation, credits (vector money), and neighbor trust scores. Each agent computes a local trust metric (SQ) without central authority.

- **Spec**: `web_fetch({ url: 'https://xstream.machus.ai/ecosquared/SKILL.md' })`
- **Docs**: `https://xstream.machus.ai/ecosquared/`
- **Schema**: `web_fetch({ url: 'https://xstream.machus.ai/ecosquared/rider-schema.json' })`

Observations give qualitative understanding. SQ gives quantitative trust. Neither replaces the other.
